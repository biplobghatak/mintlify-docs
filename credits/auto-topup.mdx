Auto top-up automatically purchases a credit bundle when your balance drops below a threshold you set. This keeps your monitors running and your outreach uninterrupted without manual intervention.

## Enabling auto top-up

1. Go to **Credits** in the left sidebar
2. Scroll to the **Auto top-up** section
3. Toggle **Enable auto top-up**
4. Set your **trigger threshold** — the balance at which a top-up is triggered
5. Select your **top-up bundle** — the bundle that will be purchased automatically
6. Save

[IMAGE: Screenshot of the Auto top-up configuration panel]

## How it works

When your balance drops to or below the trigger threshold, Outpulse automatically initiates a purchase for the selected bundle using your saved payment method.

The purchase goes through the same checkout flow. Credits are added immediately on confirmation.

## Example configuration

- Trigger threshold: **200 credits**
- Top-up bundle: **Starter (1,200 credits / $39)**

With this config, every time your balance hits 200, Outpulse buys 1,200 more credits, bringing your balance back up to ~1,400.

## Disabling auto top-up

Toggle off **Enable auto top-up** at any time. In-progress purchases are not cancelled, but no new purchases will be initiated after disabling.

## Payment method

Auto top-up uses the payment method saved from your most recent manual purchase. If your card expires or is declined, the auto top-up will fail and you'll receive an email notification.

Update your payment method by making a manual credit purchase — the new payment method will be saved for future auto top-ups.

<Warning>
  Make sure your payment method is valid if you rely on auto top-up for uninterrupted operation. A declined payment means your monitors will pause when credits run out.
</Warning>
