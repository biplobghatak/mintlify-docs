The ROI tracker on the Credits page shows you the unit economics of your X outreach compared to the industry benchmark for paid Twitter advertising.

[IMAGE: Screenshot of the ROI panel showing cost per engagement vs. Twitter Ads benchmark]

## Metrics tracked

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Credits spent** | Total credits used in the selected period |
| **Cost (USD)** | Dollar equivalent based on your bundle's per-credit rate |
| **Engagements generated** | Total replies received on your outreach posts |
| **Your cost per engagement** | Cost ÷ engagements |
| **Twitter Ads benchmark** | Industry average cost per engagement for Twitter Ads |
| **Savings vs. benchmark** | How much cheaper your Outpulse outreach is |

## What counts as an engagement

An engagement is a reply to one of your outreach posts. This is a high-quality engagement — someone taking the time to write back — not a like or impression.

Outpulse pulls engagement data from X's API 24 hours after posting.

## Why compare to Twitter Ads?

Twitter/X Ads is the most common alternative for reaching people on X. The benchmark cost-per-engagement gives you a reference point for the value Outpulse delivers relative to the paid alternative.

Outpulse outreach is typically significantly cheaper per engagement because:

1. You're reaching people who are already in a relevant conversation — not cold audiences
2. Personalized replies get higher response rates than ads
3. You're paying for AI compute and API calls, not ad auction prices

## Date range

Use the date picker to compare ROI across different periods — useful for seeing improvement over time as your tone profile and ICP are refined.

## Improving your ROI

The main levers for improving cost per engagement:

- **Raise your intent threshold** — fewer signals, but higher quality → better reply rates per post sent
- **Improve your tone profile** — more authentic drafts → higher reply rates
- **Use bulk generation** — generate multiple drafts in one session for efficiency
- **Review and skip low-quality signals** — don't spend credits on posts that clearly aren't buyers
