Outpulse can send you real-time notifications when a high-intent signal is found, so you can respond quickly without checking the inbox constantly.

Go to **Settings → Notifications** to configure.

## Slack webhook

Connect a Slack webhook to receive signal alerts directly in a Slack channel.

### Setting up a Slack webhook

1. In Slack, go to **Your workspace → Settings → Integrations → Incoming Webhooks**
2. Click **Add new webhook to workspace**
3. Select the channel you want to receive alerts in
4. Copy the webhook URL
5. Paste it into **Settings → Notifications → Slack webhook URL** in Outpulse
6. Click **Save**

[IMAGE: Screenshot of the Notifications settings page with the Slack webhook URL field]

To test the connection, click **Send test notification** after saving.

### Webhook alert format

Each alert includes:

- The monitor that found the signal
- The original post text
- The author's X handle and follower count
- The intent score
- The ICP match reason
- A direct link to the signal in your Outpulse inbox

### Removing a webhook

Clear the webhook URL field and save to stop Slack notifications.

---

## Alert threshold

The alert threshold controls the minimum intent score required to trigger a notification. Signals below this score are still saved in your inbox but won't send an alert.

**Default: 8**

Raise this if you're getting too many Slack notifications. Lower it if you want to be alerted about more signals.

<Tip>
  Set your alert threshold 1-2 points above your monitor's intent threshold. This way, only the highest-quality signals ping you immediately — you can review the rest in the inbox at your convenience.
</Tip>

## Email notifications

Email digests are sent weekly with a summary of:

- Signals found
- Drafts generated and posted
- People added
- Credit usage

You can't configure the email digest frequency — it's weekly. To stop receiving it, unsubscribe via the link at the bottom of the email.
