The posting schedule controls when queued actions are sent to X. Outpulse will only post within your configured window, regardless of when a draft was approved.

Go to **Settings → Schedule** to configure.

[IMAGE: Screenshot of the Schedule settings page showing the hours picker, day toggles, and timezone selector]

## Posting hours

Set the start and end time of your daily posting window.

**Default: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM**

Actions are queued and held outside this window. When the window opens, the queue begins processing with human-like delays between each action.

## Active days

Toggle which days of the week Outpulse should post on.

**Default: Monday – Friday**

Enable Saturday and Sunday if your audience is active on weekends or if your ICP doesn't follow a standard work week.

## Timezone

Set the timezone that your posting hours are interpreted in.

<Warning>
  Make sure your timezone is set correctly. A misconfigured timezone can cause posts to go out in the middle of the night or during hours when your audience is inactive.
</Warning>

---

## How scheduling affects the queue

When a draft is approved outside your posting window, it goes into the queue as pending. It will post at the next available time within the window.

**Example:** You approve a draft at 11:30 PM on Thursday. Your schedule is Mon–Fri, 9am–6pm (your timezone). The draft will post on Friday morning after 9:00 AM.

If your daily posting cap is hit before all pending actions are processed, the remaining actions carry over to the next posting window (not the next day — the next available window).

---

## Best practices

- **Align with your audience's active hours** — reply engagement drops significantly outside of business hours for B2B audiences
- **Avoid weekends** unless your ICP is active then — for consumer products, weekend posting often outperforms weekdays
- **Don't set too narrow a window** — a 2-hour window with a large queue may cause actions to bunch up unnaturally
