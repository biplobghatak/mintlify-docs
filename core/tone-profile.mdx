Your tone profile is what makes Outpulse drafts feel authentic. Instead of generic AI output, drafts match your sentence length, vocabulary, level of formality, and even your emoji habits.

## Setting up your tone profile

During onboarding, you'll be asked to paste 3–5 of your own posts from X. These become the training samples for the AI.

When you save your tone profile, Claude analyzes your sample posts to extract your writing patterns. This analysis costs **1 credit** and runs once per save — not on every signal or draft.

You can update your tone profile at any time from **Settings → Writing style**. Each save triggers a new analysis and charges 1 credit.

## Providing good sample posts

The quality of your samples directly affects draft quality. Here's what works:

**Use conversational posts** — replies, questions, and threads where you're engaging with someone. Conversational tone is harder to learn than broadcast-style posts.

**Include variety** — don't use 5 posts that are all the same format. Mix short and longer posts, different topics, different moods.

**Use recent posts** — your writing style changes over time. Posts from the last 3–6 months are more representative than old ones.

**Minimum 3 posts, aim for 5** — fewer samples produce less consistent results.

<Warning>
  Don't paste promotional posts or polished announcements as your primary samples. Outpulse drafts engagement responses, so it needs to learn your conversational voice, not your marketing copy.
</Warning>

## Style settings

In addition to sample posts, you can set three parameters manually:

### Formality

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Casual | Conversational, relaxed, informal abbreviations OK |
| Mixed | Context-dependent — more formal for professional topics, casual otherwise |
| Professional | Formal, complete sentences, no slang |

### Emoji usage

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| None | No emojis in any draft |
| Occasional | One emoji max per post, only when natural |
| Frequent | Emojis used liberally |

### Average post length

Set your typical post length as a rough character count. This prevents the AI from writing unusually long or short drafts compared to your style.

## Edit-and-learn

Every time you edit a draft before approving it, Outpulse records what you changed. Over time, the AI incorporates these patterns — if you consistently shorten sentences, remove certain phrases, or change a specific type of wording, future drafts will reflect that.

This happens automatically. You don't need to do anything beyond editing drafts naturally.

<Tip>
  The more you use Outpulse, the better the drafts get. The first week's drafts will need more editing than the third week's.
</Tip>

---

## Regenerating drafts

If a draft doesn't match your style, click **Regenerate** and optionally leave feedback — e.g. "too formal", "too long", "don't use bullet points". The AI uses your feedback for the next attempt.

Regenerating costs 1 credit.
