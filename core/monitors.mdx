A monitor is a saved search rule. Each monitor runs every 20 minutes and feeds matching posts into the scoring pipeline. High-intent posts become signals in your inbox.

You can create unlimited monitors per workspace.

## Monitor types

### Keyword monitor

Searches X for posts containing your query string. Use this for conversational phrases people use when they have a pain point.

**Good keyword queries:**
- `"recommend a tool for scheduling social media"`
- `"looking for a CRM alternative"`
- `"anyone tried" invoicing software`
- `"tired of" AND "spreadsheet"`

**Tips:**
- Wrap phrases in quotes for exact-match
- Use `AND`/`OR` for boolean logic
- Be specific — broad queries produce more noise and cost more credits

### Hashtag monitor

Searches posts with a specific hashtag. Best for communities that use consistent hashtags, like `#buildinpublic`, `#IndieHackers`, or `#SaaSFounder`.

### Competitor account monitor

Watches for posts that mention or reply to a specific competitor's X account. Surfaces people who are frustrated with, comparing, or switching away from a competitor — high-intent by definition.

Enter the competitor's X handle (e.g. `@competitorapp`).

### Account monitor

Watches a specific X account for new posts. Useful for tracking a potential customer, a key industry voice, or a prospect you've been watching.

## Creating a monitor

1. Go to **Monitors** in the sidebar
2. Click **New monitor**
3. Choose a monitor type
4. Fill in the query and settings (see below)
5. Click **Save**

The first scan runs within 20 minutes of creation.

## Monitor settings

### Name

A label for your own reference. Doesn't affect scanning.

### Query

The search term, phrase, hashtag, or handle. See monitor types above for guidance.

### Automation mode

Controls what happens after a signal is found. You can set different modes per monitor.

| Mode | Behavior |
|------|----------|
| **Manual** | Signals wait in inbox. You generate drafts and approve them. |
| **Smart auto** | Drafts generated automatically. AI decides which are safe to post. Rest stay in inbox for review. |
| **Full auto** | All drafts generated and posted without your approval. |

→ [Posting modes in detail](/core/posting-modes)

### Intent threshold

The minimum intent score (1–10) a post must reach to become a signal. Posts scoring below this are discarded.

**Default: 7**

- Lower threshold (e.g. 5) = more signals, more noise
- Higher threshold (e.g. 8–9) = fewer signals, higher quality

Adjust based on the signal-to-noise ratio you're seeing after the first few days.

### Scan interval

How often the monitor runs. Default is every 20 minutes. You can increase this (less frequent) to reduce credit usage on lower-priority monitors.

---

## Editing a monitor

Click on any monitor from the Monitors page to edit its settings. Changes take effect on the next scan.

## Pausing a monitor

Pausing stops scanning without deleting the monitor or its history. Use this when you want to temporarily stop a monitor without losing your settings.

## Deleting a monitor

Deleting removes the monitor and its associated signals permanently. This cannot be undone.

<Warning>
  Deleting a monitor also removes its signals from your inbox. If you want to stop scanning without losing history, pause the monitor instead.
</Warning>

## Monitor performance

Each monitor shows a signal count — the total number of signals found since creation. Use this to evaluate which monitors are producing results vs. which need query refinement.
