A monitor is a saved discovery rule. It tells Outpulse where to look for
people on X — either an account's followers/following list, or the repliers
to a specific tweet — and what filters to apply before adding them as leads.

Monitors don't send anything. They produce a pool of qualified profiles.
You then create **campaigns** against one or more monitors to actually
message people.

## Two scan modes

When you create a monitor, you pick one of two modes:

### From an Account

Outpulse scans the **followers**, **following**, or **both** of one or more
target accounts. Use this when you know an account whose audience overlaps
with your ICP — for example a competitor, a respected community leader, or
a relevant community account.

You can list multiple target handles per monitor and they're scanned as a
single pool.

**Scrape options:**

| Option | What gets scanned |
|--------|-------------------|
| Followers | People who follow the target |
| Following | People the target follows |
| Both | Union of followers and following |

### From a Post

Outpulse collects **everyone who replied** to one or more specific tweets.
Use this when a single post has a thread full of people in the exact
conversation you care about — pricing complaints, "what should I use"
threads, or product launch announcements.

Paste full tweet URLs (`https://x.com/user/status/123…`). You can list
several URLs per monitor.

---

## Filters

Both modes apply the same filters to the candidate pool:

### Bio keywords

Only keep profiles whose bio contains at least one of the listed words.

Examples: `CEO`, `SaaS founder`, `B2B`, `growth`, `agency`.

Leave empty to keep everyone.

### Follower range

- **Min Followers** — exclude profiles below this size (filters bots and
  brand-new accounts)
- **Max Followers** — exclude very large accounts that are unlikely to be
  real prospects
- **Min Following** — additional sanity filter for very low-engagement
  profiles

Set zero on any field to skip that limit.

---

## Connected X account

Each monitor is tied to one connected X account. The scanning runs on that
account's behalf. If you have multiple accounts in a workspace, pick the
one whose audience profile best matches the discovery you want to do.

---

## Creating a monitor

1. Go to **Monitors** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Monitor**
3. Choose **From an Account** or **From a Post**
4. Fill in target handles or tweet URLs
5. Set bio keywords and follower limits
6. Pick the connected X account
7. Click **Create Monitor**

Outpulse begins scanning shortly after creation. New leads stream into the
**Leads** page as they're discovered, and AI scoring runs in the background.

## Monitor lifecycle

| Action | What it does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Pause** | Stops further scanning. Existing leads remain. Resume any time. |
| **Resume** | Restarts scanning on the next interval. |
| **Delete** | Removes the monitor and its leads permanently. |

<Warning>
  Deleting a monitor also removes its leads from this workspace. To stop
  scanning without losing the lead pool, **pause** the monitor instead.
</Warning>

## How monitors feed campaigns

Monitors produce profiles. Campaigns consume them.

```
Monitor → Leads pool → Campaign import → Drafts → Sends
```

When you create a campaign, you pick one or more monitors as sources, set
an AI score threshold, and Outpulse imports the matching leads. Anyone
already imported into another campaign in this workspace is excluded
automatically.
