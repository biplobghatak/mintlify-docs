A draft is an AI-generated direct message that Outpulse creates for a specific signal. Each draft is written using your tone profile, your ICP context, and the content of the original post.

## Generating a draft

From any signal, click **Generate DM**.

Outpulse sends the post, your ICP description, and your tone profile to the AI. The result is a draft that:

- References the specific context of the original post
- Sounds like you, not like AI
- Opens a conversation rather than making a hard pitch

Generating a draft costs **0.5 credits** for a DM-only draft, or **1 credit** for a Reply + DM pair. The credit is deducted only when generation succeeds.

## Reviewing a draft

Read the draft carefully before approving. Good drafts:

- Sound like something you'd actually write
- Are relevant to the specific post (not generic)
- Invite a response rather than close a conversation
- Are an appropriate length

## Editing a draft

Click into the draft text to edit it directly. Every edit you make is recorded and used to improve future drafts (edit-and-learn).

<Tip>
  Even if a draft is 90% right, make the small edits to get it exactly right. Each edit teaches the AI something about your preferences.
</Tip>

## Approving a draft

Click **Approve** to send the draft to the posting queue. The queue handles the actual DM send to X.

→ [How the queue works](/core/queue)

## Rejecting a draft

Click **Reject** to discard the draft. The signal stays with a "rejected" status and you can generate a new draft at any time.

Rejecting does not cost credits.

## Regenerating with feedback

If a draft misses the mark, click **Regenerate** and optionally describe what was wrong:

- "Too formal"
- "Too long, keep it to 1-2 sentences"
- "Don't mention pricing"
- "More empathetic, they're frustrated"

The AI uses your feedback to produce a better next attempt.

Regenerating costs the same as generating (0.5 credits for DM-only, 1 credit for a Reply + DM pair). Each regeneration is a fresh AI call, so each one is charged.

---

## Edit-and-learn

Outpulse tracks every edit you make to a draft. Over time, it identifies patterns in your corrections:

- Consistently shortening sentences
- Removing certain phrases or words
- Changing how you open or close a message
- Adjusting formality

These patterns automatically update your tone profile. After a few weeks of use, you'll need to edit drafts less and less.

This happens passively — no configuration required.

---

## Draft quality checklist

Before approving a draft, ask:

- [ ] Does it sound like me?
- [ ] Is it relevant to the specific post?
- [ ] Does it invite a response rather than close a conversation?
- [ ] Is it the right length?
- [ ] Is it something I'd be proud to send?

If any answer is no, edit or regenerate.
