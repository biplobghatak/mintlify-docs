The **Leads** page is your workspace's lead database — every post and
profile your monitors have found, in one searchable table. Use it to triage
prospects before importing them into a campaign, or to export lead lists
for use elsewhere.

[IMAGE: Screenshot of the Leads page showing the source tabs, filter bar, and lead table]

## Two sources

The page has two tabs at the top:

| Tab | What it shows |
|-----|---------------|
| **Posts** | Individual post-level matches (a `Signal` row in the schema). Useful when you care about a specific tweet someone wrote. |
| **Profiles** | Profile-level matches (a `MonitorLead`). Useful when you care about who the person is, regardless of which post triggered the match. |

Each tab has its own filters and selection state — switching tabs clears
your selection.

---

## Filters

Both tabs support:

- **Monitor** — narrow to leads from one or more monitors
- **AI score range** — min and max (1–10)
- **Status** — filter by lead state
- **Search** — match against handle, display name, or post content

The Posts tab also exposes a **date range** filter (last 24h / 7d / 30d /
all / custom), since posts are time-bound. Profiles don't have a date
filter because they represent the person, not a moment.

Filters are mirrored to the URL — share a link to a filtered view with a
teammate and they'll land on the same query.

---

## Exporting

Click **Export** in the top-right to download leads as **CSV**, **Markdown**,
or **JSON**.

Three export scopes:

| Scope | What it exports |
|-------|----------------|
| **Filtered** | Every lead matching your current filters |
| **All** | Every lead in this workspace (ignores filters) |
| **Selected** | Only the rows you've checked |

CSV is the easiest scope for handoff to spreadsheets or other CRMs.

---

## How leads get here

Leads are populated automatically by your monitors:

1. A monitor scans X (followers/following of a target account, or repliers
   to a tweet)
2. Each profile is filtered against the monitor's bio keywords and follower
   range
3. Survivors are inserted as `MonitorLead` rows (visible in the **Profiles**
   tab)
4. Posts that match are inserted as `Signal` rows (visible in the **Posts**
   tab)
5. AI scoring runs in the background — the score appears once it finishes

You don't add leads manually. They come from your monitors.

---

## From leads to campaigns

The Leads page is the inspection layer. To actually message anyone, create
a **Campaign** from one or more monitors — that's where drafts and sends
happen.

→ [Campaigns](/core/campaigns)

## Selecting and bulk actions

Use the checkboxes on each row to build a selection. The selection persists
across pagination within a tab. From there you can:

- **Export selected** — download just the chosen rows
- Clear selection with the **Clear** action in the selection bar

<Tip>
  If you find yourself manually selecting the same kind of leads repeatedly,
  consider tightening the source monitor's filters (bio keywords, follower
  range) so the surface area starts narrower.
</Tip>
