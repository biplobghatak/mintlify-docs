Outpulse is built around a simple safety model: **you decide what to send,
Outpulse decides when to send it.** Every campaign send goes through a
per-workspace queue that paces sends, enforces daily caps, and respects
your posting schedule.

You don't configure modes per monitor anymore — campaigns are explicitly
human-driven (you pick leads, you generate drafts, you click Send).
Everything below is enforced automatically once you do click Send.

---

## Daily caps

Outpulse enforces hard daily caps per **connected account**. The caps are
tracked per (workspace × connected account × day) and reset at midnight in
your configured timezone.

| Account state | DMs / day | Replies / day |
|---------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Standard** | 30 | 30 |
| **Warm-up** (new or small accounts) | 15 | 15 |

When a cap is hit, remaining queued sends defer to the next day's posting
window automatically. They don't fail — they wait.

### Warm-up mode

Accounts under 50 followers, or in their first 14 days of activity, are
automatically placed in warm-up mode. Lower caps reduce the risk of X's
spam detection flagging a fresh account that suddenly starts sending at full
volume.

Warm-up ends automatically once the account ages out (passes 14 days **and**
exceeds 50 followers). No action required from you.

### DM cap override

In **Settings → DM limits**, workspace owners and managers can set a custom
daily DM cap if they want to send less than the default. You'll be warned
if you try to exceed the recommended ceiling, and asked to acknowledge the
risk before saving.

## Human-like pacing

When you click **Send DMs** in a campaign, Outpulse doesn't fire all the
DMs at once. Each lead gets a `scheduledAt` timestamp with a randomized
human-like delay added on top of the previous one. The queue worker picks
each row up only when its time arrives.

The result is that a 50-DM batch is spread across hours, not seconds, and
looks indistinguishable from a person clicking through their inbox.

---

## Posting schedule

Each connected account has a posting schedule (start hour, end hour,
active days, timezone). Outside of that window, queued sends wait.

Default schedule: Monday–Friday, 9 AM – 6 PM in the account's timezone.

→ [Posting schedule settings](/settings/schedule)

---

## What gets enforced on every send

Every DM passes these checks before going out:

1. **Schedule check** — within your active posting window?
2. **Daily cap check** — under the per-account DM cap for today?
3. **Account health** — is the connected account active and not paused?
4. **Per-message delay** — has the scheduled time arrived?

If any check fails, the send is deferred (not failed). Failed status is
reserved for hard errors like an X API rejection or a revoked token.

## Compliance

<Note>
  **A note on platform compliance.** Outpulse is designed for targeted,
  high-intent outreach — not mass messaging. To keep your account in good
  standing:

  - Use bio keyword filters and AI-curated import to keep your audience
    tightly relevant
  - Don't override the recommended daily caps unless you have a good reason
  - Review the first 50–100 DMs you send manually before scaling up
  - Read X's [Automation Rules](https://help.twitter.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-automation)
    before running large campaigns

  Outpulse's defaults are conservative on purpose. Treat them as a floor,
  not a starting point to push past.
</Note>
