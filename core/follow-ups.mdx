Not every reply gets a response on the first contact. Outpulse automatically monitors your sent replies and DMs and alerts you when 48 hours have passed without a response — so no warm lead goes cold without a follow-up.

## How the follow-up system works

1. A reply or DM is sent through Outpulse
2. After 48 hours, Outpulse checks whether the recipient responded
3. If they responded: the signal is marked as **responded** in People
4. If they didn't respond: the signal is flagged as **pending follow-up**
5. Outpulse prompts you to generate a follow-up message

This check runs automatically. You don't need to configure anything.

---

## Follow-up statuses

Each sent action moves through these statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `pending_check` | Sent, waiting for the 48-hour check |
| `pending_followup` | 48 hours passed, no response — follow-up recommended |
| `followed_up` | You sent a follow-up |
| `converted` | The person responded or converted |
| `no_followup` | You decided not to follow up |

---

## Generating a follow-up

When a signal is in `pending_followup` status, you'll see a **Follow up** prompt on:

- The signal in your inbox
- The contact record in People

Click **Generate follow-up** to create a follow-up draft. The AI knows the context of the original message and generates something that picks up the thread naturally — not a generic "just checking in."

Generating a follow-up draft costs **1 credit**. Posting it costs **2 credits**.

---

## Marking no follow-up

If you decide not to follow up with someone (e.g. not the right fit, conversation already concluded elsewhere), click **No follow-up** to close the loop. The signal moves to `no_followup` and won't appear in your pending follow-ups list again.

---

## Viewing pending follow-ups

From the **Inbox**, filter by **Pending follow-up** to see all contacts waiting for a follow-up. This makes it easy to batch your follow-up work once per day.

## Follow-up best practices

- **One follow-up is usually enough** — more than that risks being marked as spam
- **Add new value in the follow-up** — don't just repeat the first message; share something relevant or ask a different question
- **Timing matters** — following up 2–3 business days after the first message performs better than same-day or same-week follow-ups
