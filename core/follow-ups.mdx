Outpulse does not automatically poll X for inbound replies — conversations happen natively on X. Follow-up management is done manually through the **Outbound Tracker** pipeline status.

## The follow-up workflow

1. Outpulse sends a DM (and optional ping) to a prospect
2. The thread appears in the **Outbound Tracker** with status `Awaiting reply`
3. You check X for replies (via the **View Chat on X** deep link in the thread)
4. Update the thread status manually based on what you find

→ [Outbound Tracker](/core/inbox)

---

## Identifying threads that need follow-up

In the Outbound Tracker, filter by `Awaiting reply` to see all threads where you haven't yet updated the status. These are your candidates for follow-up.

Use the **Last outbound** timestamp to identify threads where enough time has passed — 2–3 business days is usually a good follow-up window.

---

## Sending a follow-up

Follow-up DMs are sent the same way as initial outreach:

1. Find the prospect's signal or create a new draft
2. Generate a follow-up draft (reference the prior context manually or in your tone profile)
3. Approve and queue

After sending, the new DM appears in the thread detail in the Outbound Tracker.

---

## Updating pipeline status

After checking the conversation on X, update the thread status to reflect what happened:

| Status | When to use |
|--------|-------------|
| `Replied — hot lead` | Prospect responded positively |
| `Replied — warm` | Prospect responded, interest unclear |
| `No response` | Decided not to follow up further |
| `Closed — won` | Deal closed or goal achieved |
| `Closed — lost` | Not a fit |

---

## Follow-up best practices

- **One follow-up is usually enough** — more than that risks being marked as spam on X
- **Add new value in the follow-up** — don't just repeat the first message; share something relevant or ask a different question
- **Timing matters** — following up 2–3 business days after the first message performs better than same-day or weekly follow-ups
- **Use private notes** — jot down what you know about the prospect in the thread's Notes field before writing the follow-up
