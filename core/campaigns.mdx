A campaign is the primary way you send DMs in Outpulse. You pick monitors as
sources, import their leads, generate a personalized DM for each one, and
let Outpulse send the batch at a human-like pace.

[IMAGE: Screenshot of the Campaigns list showing campaign cards with status pills and progress counters]

## How a campaign works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Import leads from monitors">
    Pick one or more monitors as sources. Outpulse pulls in matching leads,
    skipping anyone already imported into another campaign in this workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate DM drafts">
    Click **Generate Drafts** to draft a personalized DM for every pending
    lead using Claude. Each draft uses your ICP, your tone profile, and any
    campaign-specific context you've added.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and edit">
    Drafts appear card-by-card as they finish. Edit, regenerate, or skip
    individual leads before sending.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send DMs">
    Click **Send DMs** and Outpulse enqueues every drafted lead. Sends are
    paced with human-like delays and respect your daily caps and posting
    schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating a campaign

Go to **Campaigns → New Campaign**. The form has three steps:

### 1. Name your campaign

A label for your reference, e.g. "SaaS founders Q2 outreach".

### 2. Pick source monitors

Select one or more monitors as the lead source. You'll see a per-monitor
preview showing how many leads are importable at the current threshold and
how many would come in if you imported everything.

<Note>
  Leads that have already been imported into any other campaign in this
  workspace are excluded automatically — no prospect is contacted twice.
</Note>

### 3. Choose an AI score threshold

If you import in **AI-curated** mode, only profiles whose AI fit score is at
or above this threshold are pulled in. Available thresholds: 5, 6, 7, 8, 9.

| Threshold | Trade-off |
|-----------|-----------|
| 5 | Lots of leads, more borderline fits |
| 7 (default) | Balanced — good fit, decent volume |
| 8–9 | Fewer leads, highest-quality fits only |

### Import mode

Two buttons at the bottom of the form:

| Button | What it imports |
|--------|----------------|
| **Import all** | Every matching lead from the selected monitors, including unscored profiles |
| **Import AI-curated** | Only leads with an AI score at or above your chosen threshold |

The campaign is created in `draft` status and you're taken straight to its
detail page.

## Campaign detail page

Each campaign has a detail page that shows every imported lead as a card,
filterable by status. From here you generate drafts, review them, and send.

[IMAGE: Screenshot of a campaign detail page with the lead grid, action buttons, and status filter]

### Lead statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `pending` | Imported, no draft yet |
| `drafted` | AI draft generated and ready to send |
| `sending` | Enqueued for send (label "queued" internally) |
| `sent` | DM successfully delivered |
| `failed` | Send failed — re-send is available |
| `skipped` | Manually skipped or filtered out |

Use the filter pills at the top of the page to view a single status.

### Action buttons

The campaign header has four primary actions:

- **Context** — open the campaign-context modal (see below)
- **Pause / Resume** — toggle whether the campaign continues sending
- **Generate Drafts (n)** — bulk-draft every pending lead
- **Send DMs (n)** — enqueue every drafted lead

### Lead cards

Each card shows the lead's avatar, handle, bio, follower count, AI score,
and DM eligibility. Once a draft exists you can:

- **Edit** — open a full editor with character count and emoji picker; X DMs
  are plain text up to 10,000 characters
- **Regenerate** — re-draft this lead with the latest context (charges 0.25
  credits)
- **Re-send** — retry a `failed` or stuck `queued` lead

---

## Campaign context

The **Context** button opens a per-campaign instruction box for the AI. Use
it to override workspace-level framing for this specific batch — for example:

> "These leads are early-stage SaaS founders who tweeted about churn.
> Emphasize retention metrics, skip the demo offer, drop a Loom link
> instead."

Up to 2,000 characters. The workspace ICP and tone profile still apply —
campaign context only overrides them where they conflict.

<Note>
  Saving a new context doesn't re-draft existing leads. Use **Regenerate**
  on individual leads (or delete drafts and re-run **Generate Drafts**) to
  refresh them with the new context.
</Note>

## Drafting

### Bulk generate

Click **Generate Drafts (n)** to draft every `pending` lead in one batch
(up to 100 per request — the UI batches if needed). Each successful draft
charges **0.25 credits** to the workspace owner.

The page polls quietly in the background, so drafts appear card-by-card as
they finish — you don't have to wait for the entire batch.

### Single-lead generate / regenerate

On any card, click **Generate Draft** (if pending) or **Regenerate** (if
already drafted). Regeneration also costs 0.25 credits and replaces the
existing draft.

### Editing

Click **Edit** on any drafted lead to open the inline editor. The editor
includes a quick-emoji picker and character counter. X DMs are plain text:
emojis, line breaks, and links work; bold/italic formatting is stripped on
send.

---

## Sending

When you're satisfied with the drafts, click **Send DMs (n)** in the header.

What happens:

1. Outpulse creates a `QueuedAction` for each `drafted` lead, scheduled with
   a cumulative human-like delay so the batch is paced naturally
2. The campaign flips to `active` status
3. The per-workspace queue worker picks up each row when its scheduled time
   arrives, applies daily caps and schedule rules, then sends via the X API
4. **5 credits** are deducted on each successful send

Re-clicking **Send DMs** is safe — it skips leads that already have a
queued action attached (idempotent).

### Re-sending a failed lead

If a lead lands in `failed` status, click **Re-send** on its card. The send
is re-enqueued with a fresh `QueuedAction`.

---

## Pausing and resuming

Click **Pause** in the header to flip the campaign to `paused`. Already-queued
sends will still go out (they're independent rows on the workspace queue)
but no new sends will be enqueued from this campaign until you **Resume**.

Use this if you spot an issue mid-send and want to stop the bleeding while
you investigate.

---

## Deleting a campaign

From the campaigns list, click the trash icon on any card. Deleting removes
the campaign, its `CampaignLead` rows, and any unsent drafts. **DMs that
have already been sent are not affected** — they stay in your Outbound
Tracker.

<Warning>
  Deleting a campaign permanently removes all its drafts. There's no
  recovery. If you want to stop sending without losing drafts, **Pause** the
  campaign instead.
</Warning>
