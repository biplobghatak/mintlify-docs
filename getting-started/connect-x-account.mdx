---
title: "Connect your X account"
description: "How to connect, manage, and disconnect X accounts in Outpulse."
---

Outpulse connects to your X account via OAuth to post replies and send DMs on your behalf. Your credentials are never stored in plain text — access tokens are encrypted with AES-256.

<Info>
  Using Bluesky instead? See [Connect your Bluesky account](/getting-started/connect-bluesky-account) — Bluesky uses an app password rather than OAuth.
</Info>

## During onboarding

The third step of onboarding prompts you to connect your first X account. Click **Connect X account**, authorize Outpulse on X's OAuth page, and you'll be redirected back automatically.

## Adding accounts after onboarding

You can connect additional X accounts at any time:

1. Go to **Settings → Connected accounts**
2. Click **Connect new account**
3. Authorize Outpulse on the X OAuth page

Each connected account is scoped to a workspace. To use the same X account across workspaces, connect it separately in each workspace.

<Note>
  X's API limits the number of third-party apps that can be authorized per account. If you run into an authorization error, check that you haven't exceeded X's app authorization limit for that account.
</Note>

## Required permissions

Outpulse requests the following permissions when you connect your X account:

| Permission | Why it's needed |
|------------|-----------------|
| Read tweets | Fetch your own posts for tone profile learning |
| Write tweets | Post replies on your behalf |
| Send direct messages | Send DMs on your behalf |
| Read user profile | Sync your profile name and avatar |

Outpulse does not read your DM inbox or access your followers/following list.

## Pausing an account

Pausing a connected account stops all posting from that account without disconnecting it. Active monitors continue scanning and scoring, but no drafts will be posted.

To pause: **Settings → Connected accounts → Pause**

Resume at any time from the same page.

## Disconnecting an account

Disconnecting an account removes it from Outpulse and revokes its tokens. Any monitors tied to that account will be paused automatically.

To disconnect: **Settings → Connected accounts → Disconnect**

<Warning>
  Disconnecting an account does not revoke the OAuth authorization on X's side. To fully revoke access, go to **X Settings → Security → Connected apps** and remove Outpulse there as well.
</Warning>
