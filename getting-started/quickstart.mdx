This guide walks you end-to-end from signup to your first batch of sent
DMs.

## 1. Create your account

Go to [outpulse.app/signup](https://outpulse.app/signup). You can sign up
with:

- **X account** (recommended — connects your X account in the same step)
- **Google account**
- **Email and password**

<Note>
  If you sign up with email, check your inbox for a verification link
  before continuing. Google and X signups skip this step.
</Note>

After verifying, you'll land on the onboarding flow.

## 2. Name your workspace

Your workspace groups your monitors, campaigns, leads, ICP, tone profile,
and team members. The default name is "My Workspace" — rename it to match
your product or brand.

You can create additional workspaces later for separate products or X
accounts.

## 3. Connect your X account

Outpulse needs access to your X account to scan profiles and send DMs on
your behalf.

<Note>
  **X Premium is strongly recommended.** X delivers DMs from non-Premium
  accounts into a hidden "Message Requests" folder that most users rarely
  check. With Premium, your DMs land in the prospect's primary inbox with
  a push notification — significantly improving open rates. Outpulse
  works without Premium, but results will be limited.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Connect X account'">
    You'll be redirected to X's OAuth authorization page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Outpulse">
    Grant the requested permissions. Outpulse needs read + write + DM
    scope to scan and send on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to onboarding">
    You'll be redirected back automatically. Your handle and avatar appear
    as confirmation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  You can connect more accounts later from **Settings → Connected
  accounts**. Each workspace holds one X account. Workspace limits depend
  on your plan: Solo = 2 workspaces, Growth = up to 10, Agency = unlimited.
</Info>

## 4. Set up your ICP

Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) tells Outpulse who you're trying to
reach. The AI uses it to score every discovered profile.

Fill in at minimum:

- **Product name and description** — what your product does and who it's for
- **Target job titles** — e.g. "Head of Marketing", "Founder", "Growth lead"
- **Pain points** — problems your product solves
- **Keywords** — terms your ICP would use when describing the problem you solve

The more specific you are, the more accurate the scoring.

→ [Full ICP setup guide](/core/icp)

## 5. Set up your tone profile

Paste 3–5 of your own posts from X. Outpulse uses these to learn your
writing style — sentence length, tone, vocabulary, emoji usage.

<Tip>
  Use posts where you're engaging with someone, not just broadcasting.
  Conversational posts train the AI better than announcements.
</Tip>

→ [Full tone profile guide](/core/tone-profile)

## 6. Create your first monitor

A monitor finds people on X. Pick one of two scan modes:

- **From an Account** — scans an account's followers, following, or both.
  Good when you know an account whose audience matches your ICP (a
  competitor, a community leader, a relevant brand).
- **From a Post** — collects everyone who replied to one or more specific
  tweets. Good when a single thread is full of people you want to reach.

Set bio keywords to filter the pool — e.g. `CEO`, `SaaS founder`, `B2B` —
and a sensible follower range. The narrower your filters, the better
your campaigns will perform.

→ [Full monitors guide](/core/monitors)

## 7. Wait for leads to arrive

Outpulse begins scanning right after you save the monitor. Leads stream
into the **Leads** page as they're discovered, and AI fit scores appear
once scoring catches up. Most monitors produce their first usable batch
within an hour.

→ [Leads page](/core/leads)

## 8. Run your first campaign

Once you have a few hundred leads in a monitor:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Campaigns → New Campaign">
    Give it a name like "First batch — SaaS founders".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the monitor as a source">
    Select the AI score threshold (start at 7) and click **Import
    AI-curated**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate drafts">
    Click **Generate Drafts** in the campaign header. Each draft costs
    0.25 credits. Drafts appear card-by-card as they finish.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review">
    Read every draft for the first batch. Edit or regenerate any that miss
    the mark. Use the **Context** button to add campaign-specific
    instructions for the AI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    Click **Send DMs**. Outpulse paces the batch with human-like delays,
    enforces your daily caps, and only sends inside your active hours.
    5 credits per successful send.
  </Step>
</Steps>

→ [Campaigns guide](/core/campaigns)

## 9. Track replies in the Outbound Tracker

Every sent DM appears in the **Inbox** (Outbound Tracker). Outpulse doesn't
poll X for replies — click **View Chat on X** on any thread to continue
the conversation natively. Update the thread's pipeline status manually as
each prospect responds.

→ [Outbound tracker](/core/inbox)

---

## A note on compliance

Outpulse helps you reach high-intent prospects efficiently — but you remain
responsible for how you use it. X treats automated DMs to users who haven't
engaged with you first as unsolicited outreach, which can lead to account
restrictions.

**Our strong recommendation for new users:**

1. Use AI-curated import at threshold 7+ for at least your first two campaigns
2. Read every draft in the first batch — edit anything that doesn't sound like you
3. Stay at the default daily cap (30 DMs / day) until you've validated reply quality
4. Use bio keyword filters on every monitor — never run a campaign against an unfiltered pool

This protects your account and leads to better results: outreach that's
been reviewed converts better than bulk auto-sends.

---

## You're set up

From here, your typical weekly flow:

1. Top up monitors as needed (add new target accounts or tweet URLs)
2. Run a campaign once you have a worthwhile batch of new leads
3. Generate drafts, review, send
4. Check the **Outbound Tracker** for replies and update pipeline status
