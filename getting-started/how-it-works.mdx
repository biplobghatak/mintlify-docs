## Core concepts

Understanding these five concepts gives you a complete mental model of Outpulse.

### Monitor

A monitor is a saved search rule. It defines *what* Outpulse looks for on X — a keyword phrase, a hashtag, a competitor's replies, or any custom query.

Outpulse runs every active monitor every 20 minutes. A single workspace can have unlimited monitors.

→ [Create and manage monitors](/core/monitors)

### Signal

A signal is a post that matched a monitor *and* passed the intent threshold. Not every matching post becomes a signal — Outpulse scores each one first.

Signals appear with an intent score (1–10), the author's profile, and the reason it matched your ICP. Signals with a score below your threshold are discarded silently. High-scoring signals are queued for DM outreach.

→ [Outbound Tracker](/core/inbox)

### Draft

A draft is an AI-generated DM for a signal. When you click "Generate DM" on a signal, Outpulse sends the post, your ICP, and your tone profile to Claude. The result is a draft in your writing style, ready to approve or edit.

You can regenerate with feedback if the first draft misses the mark. Draft generation is free — you're only charged when the DM is sent.

→ [Drafts and AI writing](/core/drafts)

### Queue

Approved drafts go into the posting queue. Outpulse doesn't send instantly — it waits a human-like delay, applies your schedule constraints (e.g. only post Mon–Fri, 9am–6pm), and enforces daily DM caps per account.

The queue shows pending, posted, and failed actions so you always know what's happening.

→ [Managing the queue](/core/queue)

### Credit

Credits are the unit of usage. Outpulse only charges credits when a DM (or DM + ping combo) is sent — scanning, scoring, and draft generation are all free. Your monthly plan refills your credit bucket each billing cycle, and top-up packs add rollover credits that never expire.

→ [How credits work](/credits/how-credits-work)

## The automation loop

```
Every 20 minutes per active monitor:

  Monitor query
       ↓
  Fetch matching posts (TwitterAPI.io)
       ↓
  Deduplicate (48h Redis window)
       ↓
  Score each post for intent 1–10 (Claude)
       ↓
  Score ≥ threshold?
     Yes → Save as Signal
     No  → Discard
       ↓
  Automation mode?
     Manual     → Signal waits for your action
     Smart auto → AI generates draft and decides whether to send
     Full auto  → Draft generated and queued immediately
       ↓
  Posting queue
     Human-like delay
     Schedule constraints (hours, days, timezone)
     Daily DM caps enforced
       ↓
  Send to X via Twitter API v2
  Ping reply fired (best-effort) if Reply+DM mode selected
  Charge 2 credits (DM only) or 5 credits (Reply+DM) on success
```

## Posting modes

Each monitor has its own automation mode. You can run different monitors at different automation levels depending on how much you trust the context.

| Mode | What happens |
|------|-------------|
| **Manual** | Signals appear in inbox. You generate drafts and approve each one. |
| **Smart auto** | Outpulse generates drafts automatically. AI decides which ones are safe to post. You review the rest. |
| **Full auto** | Every qualifying signal gets a draft generated and posted without your input. |

→ [Posting modes in detail](/core/posting-modes)

## Account safety

Outpulse is built to protect your X account from being flagged or banned.

- **Human-like delays** between every action
- **Configurable daily DM caps** per connected X account
- **Content similarity check** before every send — never sends duplicate messages
- **Warm-up mode** for new or small accounts — starts with lower caps that increase gradually
- **Rate limiting** per connected X account

These safeguards run automatically. You don't need to configure them.
